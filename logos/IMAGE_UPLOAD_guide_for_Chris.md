# 📸 DeadDance image drive — the guide (for Chris)

You're **User #1**, and you're about to make the whole place come alive. When you add a photo to a group, it rises to the **big picture at the top** (the marquee). Fill enough of them and DeadDance stops being icons and starts being faces.

**Good news: no filenames, no folders.** Unlike the business logos, these go **straight into the app.** You open a group, tap upload, pick the photo. Done. The newest good one becomes the header.

---

## How to add a photo (60 seconds)
1. Open **deaddance.app** and **sign in** (Google or Facebook).
2. Go to the group you want to fill:
   - **Band members** — the Bloodline / lineage (Jerry, Bobby, Phil, Pigpen, Bill, Mickey, Keith & Donna, Brent, Vince, Bruce, and the rest).
   - **Jerry's guitars** — Wolf, Tiger, Rosebud, Alligator, Lightning Bolt, Top Hat, the Travis Beans.
   - **Steal Your Face — the photo wall** (the new group).
3. Tap the **photo / camera** button on that group and choose your image.
4. It uploads and jumps to the top. Add a one-line caption if you want (who/where/when).

That's the whole loop. Repeat down the list.

## What makes a great one
- **Sharp, bright, roughly square or landscape.** Faces and guitars centered.
- A little caption goes a long way — *"Jerry, Winterland '77"* or *"Tiger, up close."*
- One strong photo per group beats ten blurry ones. Quality rises to the marquee.

---

## ⚠️ Please read — keep it clean (this part matters)
These are famous people and famous instruments, so the photos usually belong to **someone** — the photographer who shot them. To keep DeadDance out of trouble, upload only:

- **Photos you took yourself** (your own shots of a show, a guitar on display, the scene). These are gold — nobody can object.
- **Public-domain or Creative-Commons images** that clearly allow reuse (check the license).
- **Images an owner gave you permission to use.**

**Please don't** pull random photos from Google Images, Getty, band/press sites, or someone's socials — those are almost always copyrighted, even when they're easy to grab.

For the **Steal Your Face** wall specifically: original fan photos (the lot, the crowd, your own tie-dye, the scene) are perfect and totally clean. The Steal Your Face skull *design itself* is a licensed Grateful Dead trademark, so lean on **your own photos**, not reproductions of the logo/art.

If you're ever unsure about one — skip it or ask Issa. Anything can be removed on request, no questions asked.
