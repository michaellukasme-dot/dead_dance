The org & how we share

dead.dance has two halves that meet in the middle — the way it already works in Santa Cruz:

🌹 The Party — the free lot, the culture, the people (Greyhound Rock, the circle of lights). Kept beautiful and free, always.  |  💵 The Business — tickets, venue subs, Shakedown, ads, festivals — the money, run cleanly around the party.

The roles map onto those two halves: Founder (Michael) holds both → the Party is held by its keepers of the flame (Chancellors) → the Business runs through the field: Ambassadors (sell) and Chaperones (ground game). Everyone shares in the revenue they help create. Titles are yours to keep — this is about function.

Michele ("Queen Bee") leads the Ambassadors nationally — all Ambassadors report to her — and holds a 2% national override, moving to a salary once revenue crosses a set threshold. Exact percentages and thresholds are in the Rep Agreement (in writing, counsel-reviewed) before anyone is paid.

Who invites whom: Michael invites the Chancellors; Michele invites the Ambassadors; an Ambassador nominates their Chaperones and Michele approves each pick before they're brought on. The family, by hand.

💡 The one big idea — you're selling the network

You are not selling a band. You're selling the network.

A venue used to chase fifty acts to fill a calendar. We hand them one relationship — the whole catalog — and the audience to go with it. That changes the pitch entirely:

Old way (selling a band)Our way (selling the network)
"Book this one Dead tribute on Saturday.""Subscribe, and fill every night — Dead & Phish tributes, originals, a comedian for a quiet Tuesday, a DJ for Friday."
One date, one check.A recurring subscription + a booking every time an act plays.
The venue takes all the risk on the draw.Our audience is already in their market and gets told about the show.

The whole pitch in one line: "Never have a dark night again." Every new act, scene, and tribute that joins the platform makes your network more valuable to sell — you have more to offer the same rooms. Use the 📡 Sell the network draft in the CRM Talent tab.

Booking acts of any genre is pure business — it lives off the Grateful Dead community feed. The Party stays the Party; the network is how the Business grows around it.

🌹 Chancellor — the job

Your scorecard

  • Member retention / active heads
  • Quality of forwarded + synthesized content

Your deliverables

  • Place the catalog — book acts of any kind into open nights (tributes across scenes, originals, comedians, DJs). More acts = more to sell to the same rooms.
  • Land festivals — get organizers running on dead.dance.
  • Recruit & lead Chaperones; keep them active.
  • Work the CRM patch — venues, bands, the Talent catalog, craft vendors, festivals, each with a ready pitch.

Your scorecard

  • Venue subscriptions sold (monthly recurring revenue)
  • Festivals landed
  • Active Chaperones recruited
  • Your chapter's attributed revenue (in the CRM rollup)

You report to Michele. Your patch and numbers roll up to her and to the Founder.

🛠️ Tool — Crowd-Forward (get FB content in)

1. Find a great post in any Dead Facebook group (a lot photo, a show review, art, a story).
2. Share / forward it to the dead.dance Facebook page — you click, you choose. We never scrape or auto-pull anything.
3. The engine reads our own page, de-duplicates (the same meme forwarded 200× becomes one item with a "shared by" count), filters out spam/ads, routes it to the right chapter, and Claude synthesizes the day's best into the Daily Synthesis.
4. A human (you, a Chancellor) approves and HyperPosts it back out — always with credit and a link home. Nothing auto-sends.

That's the whole flywheel: the family forwards, we curate, a human radiates. Your job is step 1–2, every day.

🛠️ Tool — the CRM cockpit

Open /sales_crm.html. It's your patch in one screen, organized by chapter and rolled up through the tiers.

  • 🏛 Venues — every room sorted by Dead-Scene Score; tap to draft the subscription pitch (copy-only — you send it).
  • 🎸 Bands — all the GD tribute bands in your patch; draft the sign-up invite.
  • 🎪 Craft — add local craft vendors (Chaperone's job); they fill the lot.
  • 🎆 Festivals — summer festivals to sell into; the festival pitch is ready to copy.

Every draft is copy-only — the CRM writes it, you send it (FTC-clean, human-click). Your closes are attributed to you and roll up the tiers.

🛠️ Tool — Musician Tools (the MMS)

This is the part the bands love — and our promise is simple: we give the musicians whatever they want, always.

  • Register a band + claim its page (one click, no captcha). A musician in several bands gets a badge for each.
  • Add a date — it lands on the chapter calendar and the bus map automatically; ticket-holders auto-join the show group.
  • Create a festival — a private band group + multi-day schedule + price per day.
  • Stand-ins for a single show join that band's room for the night.
  • Time, costs, P&L tracked per band — and it grows to whatever they ask for (splits, set lists, payouts, tax exports). If a band wants a change, that's the work — send it to Michael.

🛠️ Tool — the app surfaces

  • Bus map (🐯 Shows) — chapters light up by show count; tap a dark region to post the first date.
  • Calendar — "Find a Tribute Dead Show," tap a date.
  • Shakedown (🐊) — vendor booths; 👍 vote-up elevates a great local booth for free; Boost = a $15/mo sponsored ad.
  • 🍀 Miracle bucket — float a ticket you can't use; first tap grabs it; karma rules apply.
  • 📰 Daily Synthesis — Claude's read of every group, refreshed daily.
  • 🪪 You — the QR profile (your whole self in one code) + 🌹 Invite the family.

🛠️ Tool — Amplify (carry it home)

When the Daily Synthesis is good, HyperPost it out — native post in the feed, the tracked link in the first comment (beats the Facebook link penalty). And 🌹 Invite the family opens your phone's share sheet with the invite — you pick who, it never touches Facebook's API. Every post and invite carries a link that brings people home and credits the right chapter.

⚖️ The Rules (non-negotiable)

  • Every send is a human click. Nothing auto-posts, nothing is scraped, no fake accounts, no bought engagement. We curate; a person hits send.
  • Disclose when you're paid. If you're compensated and you post to promote, say so (e.g. #dead.dance partner) — it's the law (FTC) and it's in your agreement.
  • Rights, not copies. Only original community content; licensed art stays gated until the license clears. No counterfeit merch on Shakedown.
  • The family is never the product. We don't sell people's data. Ever.
  • Keep it warm, honest, peaceful. That's the whole job, underneath the tools.
