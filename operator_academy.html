The org & how we share

dead.dance has two halves that meet in the middle — the way it already works in Santa Cruz:

🌹 The Party — the free lot, the culture, the people. Kept beautiful and free, always.  |  💵 The Business — tickets, venue subs, Shakedown, ads, festivals — the money, run cleanly around the party.

Roles map onto those halves: Founder (Michael) holds both → the Party is held by its Chancellors → the Business runs through the field: Ambassadors (sell), Chaperones (ground game), and Student Chaperones (the campus co-op bench). Everyone shares in the value they help create. Titles are yours to keep — this is about function.

🎓 Student Chaperone — the job Chaperone Console ↗ campus co-op

Two seats per Ambassador region: one Undergraduate Student Chaperone and one Graduate Student Chaperone — working right alongside that region's Ambassador and its paid dead.dance Chaperones.

Your job is the Chaperone job — same work, same console. You do exactly what a Chaperone does (see above), in the same Field CRM. The only difference is how you're supervised and rewarded: you do it for credit, under two people — a faculty teacher (grades your co-op) and a chapter leader (your day-to-day mentor) — and you graduate straight into a paid Chaperone seat. (The Graduate seat typically carries more scope — mentoring the undergrad, owning a bigger slice of the patch — but the role and console are the same.)

Your deliverables — same as the Chaperone

  • Crowd-forward daily — the core habit: find the day's best group content, forward it in.
  • Sign local bands — the campus town and nearby rooms.
  • Gather craft vendors and fill the Shakedown & art tables; help staff events.
  • Welcome new heads and keep the campus/chapter feed warm.
  • Plus one student add-on: reflect & report — a short weekly log for your teacher (what you did, what you learned).

What you earn

  • Academic credit — the primary reward, through the campus co-op (undergrad & grad).
  • Achievement bounties — a set micro-reward per concrete deliverable (band signed, vendor gathered, event staffed). Deliverable-based, not open-ended sales commission — so it stays a for-credit educational role, clean with the university.
  • Perks — Miracle tickets, merch, a Family Partner reference, and tickets to every local-chapter headliner show (see below).
  • The ladder — finish the co-op in good standing → promotes to paid Chaperone with a real revenue share.

Your scorecard

Weekly content forwarded · bands signed · vendors gathered · events staffed · weekly reflection submitted · mentor + teacher sign-off.

Why it's built this way: for-credit internships have to stay primarily educational. Layering commission on top can jeopardize the credit and create a school conflict — so students earn credit + bounties + perks now, and the revenue share comes with the promotion. Terms live in the Student Co-op Addendum to the Rep Agreement.

🎟️ Headliner tickets for the local team. When we sign a headliner to play a chapter, we ask them — as part of the deal — for a block of tickets for the local team (Chaperones and Student Chaperones) to attend that in-chapter show. So the people who build the chapter get to be at the shows. The ask goes in the signing agreement; the Ambassador secures it, and seats are distributed by the chapter leader.

You are not selling a band. You're selling the network. A venue used to chase fifty acts to fill a calendar. We hand them one relationship — the whole catalog — and the audience to go with it.

🎯 Go to the Top — the Craig Benson Move

The single most valuable sales move in this house — learn it, then run it every time you're stuck. When the person who should say yes won't — a venue GM who won't return your calls, a talent buyer who ghosts, a chain that "already has a system" — you don't quit, and you don't beg the wall. You go to the one person high enough to actually decide.

The founder closed his biggest deals this way — MBNA, SAP, Delaware Group, Comcast, Meridian, AMP, and a hundred more. Same move every time. It's not a trick; it's how we sell.

StepWhat you do
1. Go upThe door's stuck at the working level? Don't lose the account — find the one person high enough to say yes (owner, GM, regional head).
2. One honest pageNot a pitch deck. One page. Name the true asymmetry — their contradiction or their opportunity — stated plainly. Leverage is a fact; say it out loud.
3. The harder truthTell the truth even when the comfortable version would close faster. "I won't oversell it — here's the real number." Being the one who told the truth is what makes it land.
4. Let the logic carry itNo begging, no hype. State what's true and let the decider see what they can't un-see. Then stop talking.

Field example. A GM won't sign the subscription and won't return calls. Don't chase the GM. Write the owner one page: "Your rooms went dark 9 Tuesdays last quarter. We fill dark nights with an audience already in your market. Your competitor two towns over just subscribed. I'd rather it were you." That's the move.

Full doctrine: THE_CRAIG_BENSON_MOVE_top_down_doctrine.md. Prove it once on one hard account, then duplicate it on every account that "can't be cracked."

🛠️ Tool — Crowd-Forward (get FB content in)

1. Find a great post in any Dead Facebook group (a lot photo, a show review, art, a story).
2. Share / forward it to the dead.dance Facebook page — you click, you choose. We never scrape or auto-pull.
3. The engine reads our page, de-duplicates (the same meme forwarded 200× becomes one item with a "shared by" count), filters spam, routes to the right chapter, and Claude synthesizes the day's best into the Daily Synthesis.
4. A human approves and HyperPosts it back out — always with credit and a link home. Nothing auto-sends.

The whole flywheel: the family forwards, we curate, a human radiates. Steps 1–2 are the daily habit — the heart of the Chaperone & Student roles.

🛠️ Tool — Musician Tools (the MMS)

The part the bands love — our promise is simple: we give the musicians whatever they want, always.

  • Register a band + claim its page (one click, no captcha). A musician in several bands gets a badge for each.
  • Add a date — lands on the chapter calendar and bus map automatically; ticket-holders auto-join the show group.
  • Create a festival — a private band group + multi-day schedule + price per day.
  • Stand-ins for a single show join that band's room for the night.
  • Time, costs, P&L tracked per band — grows to whatever they ask for. If a band wants a change, send it to Michael.

⚖️ The Rules (non-negotiable)

  • Every send is a human click. Nothing auto-posts, nothing is scraped, no fake accounts, no bought engagement. We curate; a person hits send.
  • Disclose when you're paid. If you're compensated and you post to promote, say so (e.g. #dead.dance partner) — it's the law (FTC) and it's in your agreement.
  • Rights, not copies. Only original community content; licensed art stays gated until the license clears. No counterfeit merch on Shakedown.
  • The family is never the product. We don't sell people's data. Ever.
  • Keep it warm, honest, peaceful. That's the whole job, underneath the tools.
