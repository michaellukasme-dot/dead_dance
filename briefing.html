🧭What dead_dance actually is

The Grateful Dead world is enormous and scattered — hundreds of tribute-band pages, regional chapters, taping circles, and lot crews, all spread across Facebook groups where the good stuff scrolls by once and is gone forever.

dead_dance gathers the signal. The shows, the people, the chapters, the archive — pulled into one home that feels like the lot, not like an algorithm. Behind the scenes, the noise and the ads get stripped out and the family's own content is handed back, clean. You see more of what you came for, and none of the ocean of distraction.

🌹The Family — user & community functions

Chapters & Chancellors
Regional homes — Santa Cruz (the Founders), the Bay Area, and on out. Chancellors are the heart: the people already doing the work in the scene, recruited to lead their chapter. You find your people, not a feed.
The "Every Show" cassette + "I Was There"
The archive you already loved — now it plays inside the app. Tap a show you actually caught and mark "I was there"; it joins your personal cassette and opens a room of everyone else who was there that night. Your life in shows, on one tape.
Real-time chat rooms
Magic-link sign-in (your email, no password), an 18+ gate, then chapter rooms and per-show rooms. It's live — post and the family sees it. we'll test this together
Near You + Rosebud
The app knows where you are and surfaces shows within 50 miles tonight. Rosebud — the voice — routes you in, hands-free. (This is the piece you first hit, Chris — it now asks your location, and you can set your area by hand if it doesn't. We fixed exactly that.)
Badges + the living hero
Shows attended become badges. The hero art up top rotates to honor dates and moments — it changes because it is us.

🎸Musicians — the Studio

For the tribute bands, dead_dance is a free back office bolted onto the social network. They play; the app keeps the books.

The Books & P&L
Snap a receipt, miles auto-track off the gigs, and the P&L totals month / quarter / year into one number. Tax-ready export rides along.
Your Time — the hourly engine
Log the hours behind the gig (setup, drive, teardown) at your own rate. Paid crew is a deductible expense; your own unpaid time isn't — but the app shows your true effective $/hour so you know what you're really making. Honest, not flattering.
$/Venue analysis
Which rooms pay, which are dogs, and where you're charging under the room average — money left on the table, surfaced plainly.
Booking calendar + the Ad Engine
"Check my calendar" — fans and venues request dates in one tap. And your own promo for a show surfaces to heads within 50 miles (editorial placement — your copy, your ticket link, no paid auction).

🏛️Venues — the console

The Venue Console $99 / month
The room's dashboard: bands booked, tickets sold, and heads within 50 miles of the door. The whole pitch in one glance — we fill your room with the right crowd on the right night.
One-tap booking + Shakedown
Open a band's calendar, book them in a tap. Shakedown is the vendor marketplace (the lot, formalized) with chapter and national roll-ups.

♟️Strategy & the moat

The construct is the product
dead_dance is instance #1 of a repeatable machine. The same engine runs Ford-Falcon Friends (classic cars) and TrailGummy (hikers) — and any passion world big enough to fragment on Facebook. We don't build one app; we built a factory. There are effectively unlimited 10-million-strong communities to point it at.
Ad-free, community-first
The incumbents monetize attention with ads and a subscription wall. We strip the ads and noise out of what the family brings in and put our people front and center. The thing their users are fleeing is the thing they're built on.
Facebook-friendly, by design
We don't fight Facebook — we help the family see more of the group content they care about without drowning, and link back. No violations, nowhere near the line.
Honest-state / the Jarvis rule
Nothing auto-posts, auto-charges, or auto-contacts anyone. The human always chooses. That trust posture is the brand.
It grows itself
Chancellors bring their groups; Chaperones bring the rooms; the bands bring the fans. The network's growth engine is the community itself — not a paid-acquisition budget.
