📸 Show Photos — how it works

Three ways the family makes pictures together. Teach one friend; they teach the next.

🤝Collaborate on photos with friends at shows

You don't have to be the one with the good camera. At a show, everyone's shots pool into one shared set for that night — so the best moments come from the whole crew, not one phone.

  1. Open the show in the app and tap 📷 Add my photos.
  2. Your friends do the same — everyone's shots land in the same show, pinned to where they stood.
  3. Pick the best together and build one collage. Post it to the show for everyone who was there.
Every photo is saved the instant you take it — on your phone and backed up — so nobody's night ever disappears.
🎤Stage Shot Images, explained

A Stage Shot is the crowd around the stage — not just the band. The lights, the dancers, the whole Platz, from where you stood.

  1. Face the stage, take in the whole scene, and shoot — wide, not zoomed.
  2. The app tags where you stood with GPS, so it knows your angle.
  3. Everyone's angles stitch into one view of the moment — the shot no single camera gets.
Many people, many angles, one stage, one moment. That's why we ask the whole crowd to shoot it.
👑The Chancellor's Image, explained

The big one. After the set, everyone gathers in front of the stage — the whole crew, and if we're lucky the tribute band steps in — for one group photo. One shot that says we were all here, together.

  1. As the encore ends, rally your friends to the front of the stage.
  2. Wave the band in — the best ones love it.
  3. Hand a passerby a phone (or arm's-length it), take the shot, and post it to the show.
We call it the Chancellor's Image — after Robert Garcia, the Chancellor, and the photos & the drawing that started the tradition. 🌹
🌹 Your photos are yours. Nothing posts on its own — you always choose what goes up.
